# Day 2: Variance & Standard Deviation

## 📌 Overview
On Day 2, I focused on understanding how data is spread around the mean using variance and standard deviation.

---

## 📘 Key Concepts
- Variance measures how far each data point is from the mean.
- Standard Deviation is the square root of variance and represents spread in the same unit as the data.

---

## 📐 Formulas

Variance:
σ² = (1/n) * Σ(xᵢ - μ)²

Standard Deviation:
σ = √(σ²)

---

## 🧮 Example Calculation

Dataset: [2, 4, 6, 8]

Mean = 5  
Variance = 5  
Standard Deviation ≈ 2.23

---

## 💡 Why This Matters in Machine Learning
- Helps in understanding data distribution  
- Used in feature scaling (standardization)  
- Important for identifying variability in datasets
