# Day 1: Statistics Basics for Machine Learning

## Topics Covered
- Data types (Numerical, Categorical)
- Mean, Median, Mode
- Outliers
- Normalization (Min-Max Scaling)

---

## Key Learnings

### 1. Mean
- Average of all values
- Sensitive to outliers

### 2. Median
- Middle value after sorting
- Not affected by outliers

### 3. Mode
- Most frequent value
- Useful for categorical data

---

## Outliers
- Values far from rest of dataset
- Can distort mean
- May be useful or harmful depending on context

---

## Normalization
- Scaling data to a common range (0 to 1)
- Prevents one feature from dominating others

Formula:
x' = (x - min) / (max - min)

---

## Practice Example

Dataset: 10, 20, 20, 30, 100

- Mean = 36
- Median = 20
- Mode = 20

Observation:
Mean is affected by outlier (100), median is more reliable.

---
NORMALIZATION help in equalizing the magnitude of various factor so in one factor don't dominate other based on magnitude.
